# 1-Hour Interview Timeline & Scoring Strategy

## Interview Structure (60 Minutes)

### Minutes 0-5: Opening & Rapport Building
**Objective:** Establish credibility and set tone
**Your Actions:**
- Greet warmly, confirm his role and team structure
- Deliver your positioning statement:
  > "William, I'm excited about this conversation. I've spent the last 6 years building data governance and performance reporting systems in insurance and regulated finance. What drew me to this role is that it sits right at the intersection of data quality ownership and strategic decision support—which is exactly where I've had the most impact."

---

### Minutes 5-10: Motivational Fit (1 Rating)
**Questions Expected:**
- What attracts you to Zurich?
- What interests you about this role?
- Any aspects you might dislike?

**Your Approach:**
- **Authenticity:** Be genuine about your motivations
- **Alignment:** Connect your values to the role's realities
- **Self-Awareness:** Acknowledge potential challenges (e.g., "Learning TPA domain specifics will be a curve, but I learn systems fast")

**Scoring Target:** 4-5
- Demonstrate you've researched Zurich
- Show understanding of TPA operations challenges
- Connect your governance work to their needs

---

### Minutes 10-20: Behavioral Competencies Round 1 (2 Competencies)
**Likely Competencies:** Collaborate Together + Puts Customers First

#### Collaborate Together (10 min)
**Question:** "Tell me about a time you succeeded in a project by collaborating with others."
**Your Story:** Cross-functional Strategic Initiative (Mobile App Migration)
**Key Points:**
- Emphasized multi-stakeholder alignment (Security, Legal, Audit, Vendors)
- Built governance framework to force transparency
- Delivered 95% operational readiness for 20,000+ users
**Scoring Target:** 5
- Clear direction and accountabilities ✓
- Effective utilization of others' talents ✓
- Delegated authority appropriately ✓

#### Puts Customers First (10 min)
**Question:** "Tell me about a time you took action to ensure best possible service to customers."
**Your Story:** Downstream Data Quality (83% Error Reduction)
**Key Points:**
- Framed internal stakeholders as "customers"
- Built proactive validation to prevent data trust issues
- Result: Leadership stopped asking "Is this right?" and started asking "What should we do?"
**Scoring Target:** 5
- Champions customer focus ✓
- Addresses needs proactively ✓
- Ensures outstanding experience ✓

### Minutes 45-52: Technical Competency (2 Technical Areas)

#### Technical Competency 1: Data Governance & Lineage
**Question:** "Describe what this technical competency means and why it's important."
**Your Answer:**
> "Data governance is knowing exactly where a number comes from, how it changed, and where it ends up. In TPA operations, if you can't explain variance, you can't manage performance. I use SQL and Power BI lineage to map this so we never have 'mystery numbers.' I've built validation frameworks that cut errors by 83%—that's governance in action."

**Scoring Target:** 5
- Articulates skill clearly ✓
- Evidence of expertise (83% reduction) ✓
- Successfully applied (multiple dashboards) ✓
- Proactively maintains knowledge (Python, AI exploration) ✓

#### Technical Competency 2: Performance Analytics vs. Reporting
**Question:** Same prompt
**Your Answer:**
> "Reporting tells you *what* happened. Analytics tells you *why* and *what's next.* For TPA claims, anyone can export a list. My job is to overlay trends, add benchmarks, and flag outliers so leadership can decide. I've done this at enterprise scale—15+ dashboards, 20,000+ users—and I know how to make data actionable, not just visible."

**Scoring Target:** 5
- Articulates skill clearly ✓
- Evidence of scale and impact ✓
- Successfully applied (governance framework) ✓

---

### Minutes 52-57: Your Questions for William

**Ask 2-3 High-Impact Questions:**

1. **The "Future State" Question:**
   > "William, if I've been wildly successful 12 months from now, what's the *one* thing about Claims Mart or TPA data governance that works differently than today?"

2. **The "Strategic Scrutiny" Question:**
   > "The JD mentions systematic financial scrutiny. Is the challenge that the framework doesn't exist, or that it exists but isn't being followed?"

3. **The "Ops vs. Analytics Balance" Question:**
   > "How does your team currently balance daily production reporting with deep-dive analytics? Where do you want to shift that balance?"

---

### Minutes 57-60: Close Strong

**Your Closing Statement:**
> "William, I'm confident I can deliver what you need. I bring strong data governance, proven ability to own downstream data quality, and performance reporting that gives leadership faster, more reliable insights. I'm really excited about this opportunity. What would next steps look like?"

**Listen for:**
- Any concerns about your fit
- Timeline for decision
- Additional interview rounds

## Energy & Pacing Tips

- **Stay concise:** Each STAR story = 60-90 seconds max
- **Use metrics:** Always include a number (83%, 360 hours, $5.7M, 95%, etc.)
- **Be conversational:** Don't recite; tell a story
- **Watch his energy:** If he's nodding, you're good. If he's checking notes, speed up.
- **Pause for follow-ups:** Don't fill every silence
